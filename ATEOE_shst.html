 Ambush

Tags: Action, Short Story

The man fled across the field, gun in hand, and I sprinted after him. A bullet grazed my thigh, and I dropped to the ground, rolling once. Pain seared through my leg, but I ignored it, focusing instead on the man, who was hunched over now, still running. I caught up with him, wrenched the gun from his hand, and pushed him flat on his back. He struggled, but I held him down, pressing my forearm against his throat.
"Stop fighting," I said.

He froze.

"Who sent you?"

"My friend . . . he told me about the job."

"Who is he?"

"I don't know. He just gave me an address."

"Where's that address?"

"I don't know. I just . . . he just told me to meet him here, and he gave me a gun."

"What's his name?"

"I don't know."

"Where does he live?"

"I don't know."
