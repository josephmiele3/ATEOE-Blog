On the Merits of Organization:

Tags: Personal

Writing is hard.
That’s a pretty bold statement to make, isn’t it? The thought alone can be paralyzing. What if I start and never finish? What if I hit a snag and it takes me forever? What if people aren’t interested? It’s hard enough just to get out my ideas and organize them. Putting them into a blog post or article can be a daunting task. If I’m writing something that’s meant to go viral, I have to write it quickly! Why would I write something that’s meant to last weeks or months or even years? If it’s meant to be personal, why would I make it so public?

I think this happens to a lot of people, particularly writers, and I know it’s happened to me. When I write, I like to get my ideas out. I’m a person who can’t shut up. So the words start flying and before I know it, I’m writing something that’s not quite finished. When I do that, I often just write a few sentences and stop. The words are out there, I just need to keep typing. It’s easier that way, especially when it comes to short stories, where it’s important to get to the point.

In other words, I’ve lost the forest for the trees. When it comes to writing, if I don’t organize my thoughts first, I lose the ability to do so. I lose sight of what my goal was. That's a big problem, especially if you're writing professionally.
